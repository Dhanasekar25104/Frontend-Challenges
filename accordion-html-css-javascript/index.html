Frequently Asked Question

Why is the moon sometimes out during the day?

The moon can be visible during the day because it orbits the Earth, and its position relative to the Earth and the Sun changes over time. The moon reflects sunlight, which means that when it's in the right position in the sky, it can be bright enough to be seen even when the Sun is up.

Why is the sky blue?

The sky appears blue because of Rayleigh scattering. Shorter blue wavelengths scatter more than longer red wavelengths when sunlight interacts with the atmosphere. This scattered blue light is what we see when we look up at the sky. During sunrise and sunset, the sky can look red or orange due to the longer path sunlight travels through the atmosphere, scattering more blue light out of our line of sight.

Will we ever discover alliens?

The discovery of extraterrestrial life remains one of science's biggest questions. While we haven't found definitive evidence yet, the vastness of the universe suggests that the possibility is real. Ongoing efforts, such as searching for microbial life on Mars, studying extreme environments on Earth, and scanning the cosmos for signals, aim to answer this question. Advances in technology and space exploration may one day provide the evidence we seek.

How do aeroplanes stay up?

Airplanes stay up by generating lift through their wings, which create a pressure difference between the top and bottom surfaces. Engines produce thrust to move the plane forward, allowing air to flow over the wings. Lift counteracts the weight of the plane, keeping it in the air. Effective design minimizes drag, enhancing stability and efficiency.
